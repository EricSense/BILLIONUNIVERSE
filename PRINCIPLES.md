# Principles — Billion Universe

These are non-negotiable defaults for how we design the substrate—not slogans for a slide deck.

---

## 1. Substrate before surface

Optimize the **world** first: identity, state, boundaries, and how things attach. User-facing polish follows from coherent physics. If the universe is vague, every product on top pays the tax forever.

## 2. Interoperability is structural, not optional

Tools and agents must meet **shared contracts** for context, capability, and provenance. Adapters are fine; silos dressed as APIs are not. The universe wins when composition is cheaper than lock-in.

## 3. Continuity over sessions

Intelligence is not a chat log. The substrate preserves **what persists**, **what expires**, and **who may change it**—so agents can plan, humans can audit, and systems can recover.

## 4. Accountability by design

Every consequential action has a **trace**: who or what initiated it, under which policy, with what authority. Trust scales when blame and credit are legible—not when “the model did it.”

## 5. Governance is part of the physics

Rules are not an afterthought bolted onto “the AI.” Permissions, escalation, and human override are **first-class mechanics**—as fundamental as storage or messaging.

## 6. Human and artificial intelligence share the same floor

Same affordances, same constraints, same audit story where it matters. The universe does not fork into “for people” and “for machines” at the root.

## 7. Minimize magic, maximize explicitness

Defaults should feel good; **surprises** should be rare. When behavior is implicit, document it in the system—not only in marketing.

## 8. Scale coherence, not just scale usage

A billion actors are useless if they cannot agree on what is real. We invest in **shared semantics**, conflict handling, and evolution of the world model—not only raw throughput.

---

*Principles drift; when they conflict with shipping, we revise the principles in the open—or we are building another wrapper, not a universe.*
